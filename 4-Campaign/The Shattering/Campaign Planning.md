Plot: **A frontier cold war that escalates into a Dungeon-driven conflict** Roughly 20 years after the Sundering the world is just starting to get back on it's feet and it seeks to understand the strange, broken place it has become.

# ⚔️ CAMPAIGN FRAMEWORK

## **“Embers Between Two Lights”**
Found in the Prismatic Canopy the two cities. One, a survivor of the Shattering, hanging on through strict Order and discipline.  The other, new and embracing the Chaos of the new world seeking to find harmony and unafraid of discovery.  The one unknown and new Dungeon. The first true prize of the new world.

### THE TWO CITIES
These need to feel _philosophically opposed_, not just politically.

### 🏛️ Castlemere (The Survivor)
**“We endured. We will not fall again.”**

**Castlemere Map:** https://watabou.github.io/city-generator?size=20&seed=2246299&name=Castlemere&citadel=1&urban_castle=0&plaza=1&temple=1&walls=1&shantytown=0&coast=0&river=0&greens=0&gates=-1

A remnant city that survived the Shattering. Castlemere is defensive, cautious, structured.  It values stability over expansion and harbors a deep fear of unknown forces.  The people of Castlemere believe The Shattering was a warning.  That the world is still unstable and that survival requires control.  The community is strengthened by their defensive infrastructure, their experienced leadership and rigid adherence to organization.  That, however, makes them exceptionally risk-adverse, slow to adapt and paranoid.  Their initial view of the Dungeon is, “It is a threat. If it spreads, we may not survive a second collapse.” Castlemere will see the Dungeon as a threat to contain and will seek to possibly to seal or destroy it.  They see "Dungeon-trait" items as corrupted or dangerous, viewing the players as becoming "part of the wound".

**Castlemere NPCs** 
- “It’s a wound in the world.”
- “It must be contained before it spreads.”

### 🏗️ Springrun (The New City)
**“The world is broken. That means it can be rebuilt.”**

**Springrun Map:** https://watabou.github.io/city-generator?size=19&seed=2111741135&name=Springrun&citadel=0&urban_castle=0&plaza=1&temple=1&walls=0&shantytown=0&coast=0&river=1&greens=1&gates=-1

A newly founded settlement pushing outward.  Springrun is ambitious, adaptive, opportunistic.  Built by survivors who refused to hide and willing to take risks in the name of progress and opportunity. The people of Springrun believe The Shattering created new opportunities.  That power belongs to all and that the unknown is meant to be explored.  The community is strengthened by their flexibility, innovation and bold, if young, leadership.  This makes them a bit lacking in structure.  Their leadership is somewhat overconfident and there are small amounts of internal division and strife.  Their initial View of the Dungeon is,  “This could be the greatest discovery since the Shattering.”  Spring will see the Dungeon as a resource to explore and possibly to harness. They see "Dungeon-trait" items as the "greatest discovery since the Shattering" and pressure the players to bring more back for study and replication.

**Springrun NPCs**
- “It’s a source of endless resources.”
- “We just need better strategy.”

## PLAYER ROLE: THE PIVOT POINT
The players, having been sent a summons for assistance by both towns, have chosen to accept the summons from Springrun.

## 🌲 THE DEEP WILDS DUNGEON: “The Verdant Hollow”

This is the heart of everything. Approaching The Verdant Hollow there should be strange distortions of the natural world around it as the mere presence of the Dungeon begins to distort reality.

The Verdant Hollow should feel organic in nature.  On the surface it appears to be a pre-Shattering old forest with mature trees and flora.  Upon closer inspection, some of the flora is new and different.  Resources to be potentially exploited by the resourceful. The fauna here again, should be similar to pre-Shattering forest creatures.  However, some creatures are significantly more hostile and aggressive.  Giving the "the hollow" a more pre-historic kind of feel and flavor.  Not populated with Dinosaurs however.  A Dungeon should feel structured but not built.

### First Discovery

The party is the first organized group to enter and should be the first to return with coherent information.  At this point in the continuity, Dungeons are first being discovered and understood.  No one knows any of their secrets like, it’s a pocket dimension, has a core or “Seed” and that it regenerates.

The players should be able to observe some of the oddities of Dungeons.  If they spend enough time exploring they may see creatures reappear over time and that paths subtly change.  Some areas feel “deeper” than they should.  Creatures which turn into dust and some which leave behind strange items (equipment, quintessence, awakening stones and essences).  They're normal weapons and abilities will be highly ineffective.
