# 1. **The Ember Frontier**

**Core Theme:** Founding a settlement at the edge of the unknown  
**Tone:** Survival, exploration, slow expansion

A young kingdom pushes outward, establishing a frontier settlement in a region warped by the Shattering. Strange geography, unstable magic, and the **first Dungeon manifestation** appear nearby.

### Campaign Spine

The party are **charter agents**—tasked with:

- Securing the region
    
- Mapping terrain
    
- Protecting settlers
    
- Investigating anomalies
    

### Dungeon Role

The Dungeon is:

- Initially treated as a **hazardous ruin**
    
- Then a **resource**
    
- Then something clearly _alive or reactive_
    

### Arc Progression

- **Lv 5–7:** Establish the settlement, first expeditions
    
- **Lv 8–10:** Expansion, threats, competing settlers
    
- **Lv 11–13:** The Dungeon anchors fully and reshapes the region
    

### Why This Fits

This is your **purest “points of light” campaign**:

- The map literally fills in as you play
    
- The party defines what “safe” means

# 2. **Road of Broken Sky**

**Core Theme:** Reconnecting isolated enclaves  
**Tone:** Journey-based, episodic exploration

After the Shattering, pockets of survivors exist—but **no safe routes connect them**.

The party is hired to **chart and secure a stable overland route** between two distant settlements.

### Dungeon Role

Dungeons appear:

- Along leyline fractures
    
- As obstacles blocking travel
    
- As shortcuts (dangerous ones)
    

### Arc Progression

- **Lv 5–7:** Establish safe passage segments
    
- **Lv 8–10:** Encounter first stable Dungeon corridors
    
- **Lv 11–13:** Discover a Dungeon that _is_ a road
    

### Why This Fits

- Travel-focused, discovery-driven
    
- Introduces Dungeons as **geographic features**, not just locations

# 3. **The Valley That Wasn’t There**

**Core Theme:** Discovery of newly formed land  
**Tone:** Wonder → unease → transformation

A valley appears where none existed before—a result of the Shattering settling.

Inside:

- New ecosystems
    
- Strange creatures
    
- A Dungeon forming at its center
    

### Campaign Spine

The party leads one of the **first sanctioned expeditions** into this region.

### Dungeon Role

The Dungeon:

- Grows over time
    
- Changes the valley’s ecology
    
- Attracts creatures and settlers alike
    

### Arc Progression

- **Lv 5–7:** Initial exploration
    
- **Lv 8–10:** Competing expeditions arrive
    
- **Lv 11–13:** Valley becomes permanently altered by the Dungeon

## 5. **The First Expedition Charter**

**Core Theme:** Organized exploration begins  
**Tone:** Professional explorers in a chaotic world

A ruler or mage council issues one of the **first official exploration charters**.

The party is among the first **recognized expedition teams**.

### Campaign Spine

- Survey unknown territory
    
- Record findings
    
- Return alive (optional 😄)
    

### Dungeon Role

- Logged, categorized, poorly understood
    
- Different Dungeons behave differently
    

### Arc Progression

- **Lv 5–7:** First expeditions
    
- **Lv 8–10:** Patterns begin to emerge
    
- **Lv 11–13:** A major discovery challenges all assumptions

## 6. **The Cradle of Storms**

**Core Theme:** Elemental instability post-Shattering  
**Tone:** Environmental danger, high tension

A region of constant storms stabilizes just enough to explore.

Inside lies:

- Elemental phenomena
    
- A storm-wrapped Dungeon
    

### Campaign Spine

The party investigates:

- Why the storms are calming
    
- Why they’re returning
    

### Dungeon Role

- Acts as a **pressure valve** for magical energy
    
- Its state directly affects the region

## 9. **The Horizon Project**

**Core Theme:** The first long-distance exploration effort  
**Tone:** Big expedition, multi-phase

With ships not yet viable, a kingdom launches a **massive overland expedition** to push farther than ever before.

### Campaign Spine

- Multiple camps
    
- Supply lines
    
- Unknown threats
    

### Dungeon Role

- Waypoints, hazards, and mysteries
    
- Some are avoidable—others unavoidable
