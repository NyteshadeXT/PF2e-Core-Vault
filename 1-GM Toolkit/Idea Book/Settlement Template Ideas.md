## Qualities

### Disciplined

Castlemere maintains strict civic order and highly organized defensive practices. Guards are well-drilled, patrols are frequent, and emergency protocols are deeply ingrained among the populace.

### Fortified

Though damaged during the Sundering, Castlemere’s stone walls and central keep remain among the strongest defensive structures in the region. Extensive reconstruction efforts continue throughout the settlement.

### Survivor’s Resolve

The people of Castlemere endured the Sundering firsthand. Hardship, rationing, and sacrifice are accepted as necessary realities of survival.

---

## Disadvantages

### Isolationist

Travel into and out of Castlemere is heavily monitored. Outsiders are viewed cautiously, and information regarding threats in the surrounding wilderness is often restricted.

### Resource Scarcity

Despite ongoing recovery, food stores, workable metal, and skilled labor remain limited. Much of the city still bears visible scars from the Sundering.

### Authoritarian Rule

The Magistrate Council values stability above personal freedoms. Curfews, mandatory labor levies, and strict regulations are commonplace.

## Defenses

Castlemere is defended by:

- Reinforced stone walls in various states of repair
- Watchtowers and signal beacons
- A disciplined standing militia
- The castle-fortress overlooking the settlement

Construction crews work constantly to reinforce breaches and restore damaged battlements left from the Sundering.

## Lore

Castlemere stands as one of the oldest surviving settlements in the region surrounding the **Prismatic Canopy**. Though heavily damaged during the Sundering, its walls held when countless other communities fell. In the years since, the city has rebuilt slowly and deliberately, valuing order and stability above all else.

Life within Castlemere is tightly structured. Citizens accept hardship as the price of survival, and many genuinely believe the city’s uncompromising discipline is the only reason it still stands. Yet beneath that stability lies fear—fear of another collapse, fear of the unknown wilderness beyond the walls, and increasingly, fear of the strange phenomena emerging from the Deep Wilds.

The recent discovery of an unnatural place deep within the forest has divided the city’s leadership. Some argue it must be studied carefully. Others believe it should be sealed or destroyed before it threatens the fragile balance of the recovering world.

## Notable Locations

### The Castle

A partially restored fortress overlooking the city. Seat of the Magistrate Council and headquarters of Castlemere’s military forces.

### Silverwalk

The primary market district, built around older surviving streets from before the Sundering.

### Misty Market

A crowded district of laborers, salvagers, traders, and ration merchants.

### Light Edge

A poorer district nearest the outer breaches in the wall where reconstruction efforts remain ongoing.

### Milk Crest

A farming and livestock district beyond the northeastern gate supplying much of the city’s food.
