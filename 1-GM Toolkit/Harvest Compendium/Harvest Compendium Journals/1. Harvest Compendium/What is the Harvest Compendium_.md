# What is the Harvest Compendium?
No one in the world needs loot more than a band of adventurers. The Harvest Compendium answers this need by providing unique trophies, hides, organs, and other monster bits from the creatures an adventuring party may encounter on their travels.

Each creature entry in the Harvest Compendium is unique, though the items that appear in the entries can be repeated if the creature is part of a specific group. For instance, all creature entries for dragons provide dragonhide, just as all ghostly creatures can be harvested for ectoplasm.

The Harvest Compendium consists of two main parts: the creature entries and the appendices. The creature entries list the loot available from each creature. Sometimes these items have full descriptions; other times they are simply a header referencing a preexisting item in official material or in the appendices. The appendices contain a variety of the new craftable loot, such as oil of fire coating, lucky feathers, and various armors made from monster hides.

## Using Harvest Compendium at the Table

The intention of the Harvest Compendium is to provide a satisfying, specific loot that correlates with the enemy the party just finished fighting. The loot of each creature entry has been balanced to provide items of monetary value equal to the creature’s level, with very few exceptions.

Some creatures—particularly rare and unique creatures—may provide loot that is more valuable than the standard haul for a creature of that level. In the same vein, some creatures have entries that provide loot of less monetary value than what is suitable for their level. Most humanoids fall into this category, as humanoids can rarely be harvested for anything other than the equipment they are wearing.

All the new mechanics for using the Harvest Compendium are found in this journal, but the GM can modify or even ignore these rules in favor of a style that better fits their table. Some tables might look past the harvest time requirements in favor of a speedier playstyle, while other groups might prefer to stick to the rules as written. The GM can also choose to use unique pieces of creature loot to spice up the wares of stores and shops.

The Harvest Compendium is a tool that should add to a campaign, not control it. Use the compendium in a way that best enhances play at your table, even if that means breaking the “rules.”
