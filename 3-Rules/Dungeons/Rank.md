Dungeon Rank serves several simultaneous purposes:

- Communicate expected danger
- Establish regional/world significance
- Define how much a Dungeon can distort reality
- Determine economic and political importance
- Set expectations for resource quality and/or rarity
- Establish Seed potency
- Help guide PF2e encounter math and campaign structure

The key is that Dungeon Rank should _not_ simply equal creature level. A high-rank Dungeon can still contain low-level regions, and a low-rank Dungeon can contain isolated apex threats. Instead, Rank defines the _ceiling and scale_ of the Dungeon as a phenomenon.

# Dungeon Rank Structure

## Lesser Dungeon
The most common “stable” Dungeons discovered near civilization. These are dangerous but survivable by organized adventurers and are often the first Dungeons explored by settlements or guilds.

### PF2e Mechanical Range
- Most active regions usually align with Levels 1–5
- Hidden apex zones may exceed this significantly: up to Level 7
- Typical Party Tier: Early Heroic play
- Can contain _any_ creature level in isolated circumstances

### Characteristics
- Limited resonance intensity
- Effects mostly contained internally
- Seed influence weak or immature
- Minor environmental leakage
- Valuable but not transformative resources
- Dangerous primarily to individuals or settlements

### Examples
- Abandoned ruins with resonance corruption
- Small elemental caverns
- Predatory fungal labyrinths

---

## Greater Dungeon
Major regional Dungeons capable of sustaining entire economies or threatening nearby settlements. These are large-scale expedition sites requiring preparation, logistics, and often faction involvement.

### PF2e Mechanical Range
- Most explored regions often Levels 6–10
- Apex threats may dramatically exceed expectations: up to Level 12
- Typical Party Tier: Mid-level Heroic play
- Can include lower “harvest zones”

### Characteristics
- Strong resonance expression
- External influence measurable across a region
- Significant mutation/ecological effects
- Major economic or military importance
- Stable apex ecosystems
- Seed exhibits adaptive behavior

### Examples
- Massive volcanic caverns
- Floating crystalline ruins
- Endless fungal undercities
- Ancient war-machine complexes

---

# High Dungeon
Reality-warping megadungeons whose existence reshapes entire regions. These are the kinds of Dungeons nations care about.

### PF2e Mechanical Range
- Common active regions often Levels 11–15
- Apex Threats: up to Level 17
- Typical Party Tier: Paragon-style play

- 
- Internal rules may invalidate normal assumptions
- High variance between zones

### Characteristics
- Dungeon laws begin overriding natural laws
- Space/time distortion possible
- Large-scale environmental corruption
- Long-term exposure alters creatures permanently
- Entire civilizations may organize around or against it
- Seed exhibits semi-sentient behavior

### Examples
- Skyborne labyrinths
- Endless mirrored cities
- Living storms
- Reality-folding observatories

---

# Cataclysm Dungeon
Rare world-threatening Dungeons tied directly to the Shattering, Primordials, dead gods, or cosmic forces. These are existential threats.

### PF2e Mechanical Range
- Typical Creature Levels: 16–20+
- Apex Threats: 21–25+
- Typical Party Tier: Legendary play
- Encounter ranges become extremely flexible
- Entire campaigns revolve around containment or survival
- Mechanics may intentionally break PF2e norms

### Characteristics
- Threatens nations or reality itself
- Seed behaves as an active cosmic force
- Dungeon may spread autonomously
- Outside world increasingly assimilated
- Divine/Primordial influence common
- Dungeon may possess true intelligence

### Examples
- Shardfall craters
- Primordial prisons
- Infinite void structures
- Living divine corpses
