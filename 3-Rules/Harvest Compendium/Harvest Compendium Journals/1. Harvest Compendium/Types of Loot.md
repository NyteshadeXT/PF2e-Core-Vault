# Types of Loot
There are many types of loot a lucky adventurer might discover on a dead foe. Some pieces of loot can be picked up and used immediately or after simple repairs, while others require a proficient carver to successfully harvest and process before they are useful.

> **Crafting Items Not from the Core Rulebook**
> 
> This compendium and the creature entries within have been written based on information found mainly in the Pathfinder Second Edition Core Rulebook. Many magic and alchemical items exist in other Pathfinder Second Edition books where certain ingredients would be suitable inserts for crafting the item. For example, a vial of [[Pure Light|Pure Light]] could be used to craft a [[bottled-sunlight-lesser|Bottled Sunlight]]. The Harvest Compendium can’t take all existing items from the Pathfinder Second Edition roster into account, but GMs are encouraged to allow their players to use the ingredients and creature components herein to craft items taken from other books and adventure paths.

## Broken Items

In the heat of battle, weapons and armor are subject to heavy damage, leaving them unusable until repaired. The [[Broken]] items found in this compendium have Hit Points equal to their Broken Threshold, unless determined otherwise by the GM.

## Creature Components

Creature components can be used as, and sometimes be crafted into, unique equipment. [[Actions/Craft|Crafting]] something from creature components follows the standard crafting rules found in the Core Rulebook and always has the component as a craft requirement. The listed price of the creature component is subtracted from the raw material cost needed to craft the item. The GM can switch a specific creature component for another suitable material to better suit their game.

Some components act as consumables that allow you to cast a spell. Unless stated in the item, you do not need to have a spellcasting ability to use the item in this way, provided all other requirements for using the item are met.

Creature components cannot be crafted, only harvested.

## Ingredients

Ingredients are a category of item that can be crafted into something else, replacing some or all the material costs associated with crafting that item. The ingredient is consumed in the crafting process.

In crafting, ingredients are used in place of other materials. These ingredients are presented in units, the nature of which differs depending on the ingredient’s properties and how it should be stored. For instance, liquids are measured in vials, whereas powders are measured in pouches. A character must have a suitable container for these ingredients when they harvest a creature.

When crafting with ingredients, you use ingredient units equal to half the item’s level rounded up. These supplied ingredients replace the raw materials you supply to begin crafting the item. For instance, crafting a [[potion-of-resistance-(lesser)|Potion of Resistance]] requires 3 units of an ingredient for a 6th-level potion, 5 units for a 10th-level potion, and so on. Crafting a flask of [[alchemists-fire-lesser|Alchemist's Fire]] requires 1 unit of an ingredient for a 1st-level flask, 2 for a 3rd-level flask, and so on.

If the GM allows it, ingredients can be traded with artisans for a suitable item. If a party has gathered six vials of [[Angel Blood]] and wants to turn them into [[healing-potion-minor|Healing Potion]], but they don’t have time to craft them, they can trade the ingredients for the item directly. However, doing so requires ingredient units equal to the item’s level rather than half its level. An exception is items that are 1st-level items, where 2 ingredient units are required. Using the above example, the party can trade their six vials of angel blood for one moderate healing potion, or they can spend 4 days using the ingredients and some gold to potentially craft two moderate healing potions instead.

Ingredients cannot be crafted or bought, only harvested.

> **Crafting with Ingredients Example**
> 
> Halina is a 10th-level ranger with the expert rank in Crafting. She has a modifier of +14 and she has the [[Feats/Magical Crafting|Magical Crafting]] feat. She has nine pouches of [[Lightning Concentrate|Lightning Concentrate]] and wants to use them to craft some 6th-level [[potion-of-resistance-(lesser)|Potion of Electricity Resistance (Lesser)]]. The GM secretly sets a DC of 22.
> 
> As the potion is a consumable, Halina decides to craft a batch of four potions. The nine pouches of lightning concentrate act as raw materials for three of the potions, and Halina expends 22 gp and 5 sp on raw materials for the fourth potion in the batch. After 4 days of work, Halina attempts a Crafting check. She rolls a 10 on her Crafting check for a total of 24. A success!
> 
> Rather than spend any more time crafting the items, Halina decides to pay the remaining material cost of 90 gp to have the potions finished immediately. By using the lightning concentrate, Halina saved 77 gp and 5 sp on crafting the potions.

## Rune Frameworks

Frameworks supply some basic components needed for etching a rune into a piece of equipment, affording you a discount on the total cost of materials. These frameworks look like miniscule runic symbols either grafted into a creature’s hide, scales, or bone, or sometimes etched onto the borders of armor or weapons.

You can only apply one framework while etching a rune. Unlike the price of creature components, the price of frameworks does not count towards the total price of raw materials for crafting the rune. For instance, if a framework is worth 50 gp and it provides a 10% discount, the cost of the listed rune is reduced by 10%, not by 50 gp.

Frameworks cannot be crafted or bought, only harvested.

## Trophies

Some creatures can be harvested for trophies, these being items that have no crafting or utility benefits for an adventurer. They have no value other than as trade goods, except for providing bragging rights.

Trophies cannot be crafted, only harvested.

## Existing Items

Some creature loot references items already listed in the Core Rulebook or similar material. Examples of this include purple worm venom and lich dust. In cases where such loot would be found (besides armor, weapons, or basic adventuring gear), reference to the relevant material has been written by the item.
