# Harvesting Creatures
The means by which a character can harvest a creature depends on the skill of the character and the state of the creature. Anyone can pick up a broken sword and cut off a head, but only a skilled butcher can carve the hide from a dragon.

If you meet the requirements, you can attempt to harvest a creature for its available loot using the [[Harvest Creature]] activity.

Without the appropriate tools, only part of a creature’s loot can be obtained. Items such as broken weapons and armor can simply be picked up, but fully harvesting a creature requires skill and finesse. This level of precision requires the use of a type of artisan’s tools called a [[carvers-kit|Carver's Kit]], a toolkit that consists of scrapers, a mincing blade, bonesaws, and several fine knives.

A carver’s kit can be bought for 4 gp.

## Harvest Time

The time it takes to harvest a creature is determined by its size. If a character is unable or unwilling to harvest for the total duration, their degree of success on the Crafting check made to harvest the creature is lowered by one step. However, a character must spend at least half the listed time harvesting a creature for the chance at any loot.

### **Time It Takes to Harvest**

| Size | Time |
| --- | --- |
| Tiny | 10 minutes |
| Small | 30 minutes |
| Medium | 1 hour |
| Large | 2 hours |
| Huge | 4 hours |
| Gargantuan | 8+ hours |

The listed times assume a character wants to get as much loot as possible. If a character only wants a specific item, such as a vial of fresh basilisk blood to save an ally from being petrified, the GM can shorten the total harvest duration. The GM can likewise lengthen the harvest duration if they deem it appropriate.

## State of the Creature

If a creature has been rotting away for several weeks, there is less to harvest than if it was freshly slain. It’s up to the GM to determine whether a certain type of loot can be harvested based on the stage of decay. For instance, a boar that has been dead more than a day cannot be harvested for rations, and the skeleton of a dragon doesn’t have any hide on it.

The GM can add penalties to Harvesting a Creature if the corpse is severely damaged, has been exposed to the elements for a long period of time, or if harsh weather makes the process of harvesting difficult.

## Humanoid Foes

Creatures with the humanoid tag cannot be looted in the same manner as other creatures. This document provides some loot that can be taken from these enemies, such as weapons, armor, and other equipment, but no items that would need to be “harvested,” such as blood, skin, or organs. That method of looting is reserved for non-humanoid creatures, meaning this compendium doesn’t supply things like bugbear teeth or a medusa’s head.
