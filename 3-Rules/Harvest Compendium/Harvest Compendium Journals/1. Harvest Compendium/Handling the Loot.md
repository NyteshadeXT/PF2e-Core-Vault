# Handling the Loot
Once you’ve gotten the loot, you have to figure out how to use it. Most creature parts need to be processed in some way before they can be used, and only few items can be used right after harvesting.

## Optional Rule: Hyper-Focused Craft

To speed up the crafting process and allow a party to make items while on the move rather than during downtime only, the GM can introduce the Hyper-Focused Crafting action to the game. This allows characters to start or make headway on a crafting project without having to dedicate an entire day to the endeavor, though with certain drawbacks.

### Crafting Trained Action: [[Hyper-Focused Craft|Hyper-Focused Craft]]

exploration

manipulate

You spend 1 hour making headway on a project to [[Actions/Craft|Craft]] an item or items. During this hour, you focus entirely on your work and can’t do anything else. If you are interrupted during this time, you automatically fail the action.

This action is an extension of the Craft trained action, and it follows the same requirements. At the end of the hour, you make a standard DC `Crafting check` check for the level of the item you’re trying to make.

* * *

**Success** You make progress on your project equal to 1 day of work. You are [[Conditions/Fatigued|Fatigued]]. You cannot use any exploration actions while fatigued in this way.

**Failure** You make no progress on your project, but you don’t waste any materials. You are fatigued. You cannot use any exploration actions while fatigued in this way.

## Edible Items

Edible items, like raw meat or an angel’s divine tongue, must be cooked or cured before they can be safely consumed. If you try to consume an edible item that has not been cooked or cured, you must succeed at a standard DC Fortitude save for your level or become sickened 1 for 1 hour. You can cure edible items using the Cure Food trained action.

### Survival Trained Action: [[Cure Food|Cure Food]]

exploration

maniplation

**Requirements** You are holding or wearing cookware.

* * *

You spend 1 hour curing a fresh edible item, such as the heart of a great boar or the tongue of an angel, or up to 10 Bulk of raw food, such as meat taken from a slain animal. For an item to be considered fresh, it must be less than 8 hours since it was harvested (though spells like gentle repose can extend this duration). You must have a fire available for the duration of the curing process.

* * *

Roll a `Survival check` check. The GM sets the DC, which is usually a standard DC for the item’s level.

**Success** The item is cured and has an expiration date of 1 year.

**Failure** The item is ruined and cannot be used.

## Unprocessed Items

Unprocessed items do not function until they have been alchemically refined. These are typically venoms and poisons harvested directly from a creature. To make them function as an injury-based poison, they must be stabilized using a set of alchemist’s tools and the Process Item trained action.

### Crafting Trained Action: [[Process Item|Process Item]]

exploration

manipulate

**Requirements** You are holding or wearing a set of alchemist’s tools.

* * *

 You spend 1 hour refining a venom, poison, or similar item harvested from a creature, thereby removing the unprocessed trait from the item and making it useable.

The GM sets the DC, which is usually a standard DC for the item’s level.

**Success** The item’s unprocessed trait is removed, and it is now useable.

**Failure** The item is ruined and cannot be used.  

## Price

Creature components and ingredients cannot be purchased in stores. The price listed on each loot entry is there as a guide for adventurers who want to get rid of these items by selling them. An item can be sold for half its listed price, or at full price if working under contract.

For instance, an adventurer might pick up a contract for an ankhrav hive mother mandible and, after successfully slaying and harvesting the creature, turn in the mandible for its full 50 gp value. However, the adventurer also got 10 vials of impure acid from the ankhrav hive mother, each of which has the listed price of 1 gp. Because the acid was not part of the contract, the adventurer is only able to sell it for half price, netting an additional 5 sp per vial.

## Formula, Rarity, and Raw Materials Cost for New Items

This compendium offers many new magic items, oils, and potions for the intrepid adventurer. To make these items, their formula must be known, but given that the items are often uniquely tied to specific creatures, formulas are often hard to find.

If a magic item has the uncommon rarity, its formula is only known by expert hunters who have studied the slain creature for years. These artisans can be found in hunting guilds where they make a livelihood out of crafting goods from slain monsters. Some of them may be willing to teach an adventurer a few secrets of their trade, or at the very least act as an intermediary artisan for them—for a price, of course. Magic items with no rarity are more easily crafted and their formulas are available for purchase in many major cities.

If you can craft an item into a weapon or set of armor that has a rune pre-installed, you need to know the formula for both the item and the rune. For example, if you are crafting a [[Equipment/Maul|Maul]] out of a [[Brain Collector Claw|Brain Collector Claw]], you need the formula for crafting a maul and the formula for the [[Equipment/Striking|Striking]] rune. You must meet the level requirement and monetary cost for crafting both the item and the rune.

> **Crafting Equipment with Pre-Etched Runes**
> 
> Felix is a 5th-level wizard who has the expert rank in Crafting and who is looking at a few weeks of downtime. He has a modifier of +10 and he has the [[Feats/Magical Crafting|Magical Crafting]] feat. He has a [[Brain Collector Claw|Brain Collector Claw]] in his possession and wants to craft a [[Equipment/Mace|Mace]] out of it. The GM secretly sets a DC of 19.
> 
> Crafting a mace out of the claw means that it comes with a [[Equipment/Striking|Striking]] rune already etched into it when completed, but Felix still needs the formula and the materials for the striking rune. He must spend half the value of the mace (1 gp) and the 4th-level striking rune (65 gp) to attempt to craft the item, coming out to 33 gp. However, the brain collector claw has a material cost of 30 gp, lowering the required investment to 3 gp.
> 
> Paying the cost, Felix spends 4 days working on the mace and attempts a Crafting check. He rolls a 13 on his Crafting check for a total of 23. A successful check!
> 
> Felix decides to spend another 10 days working on the item, reducing the cost by an additional 10 gp, before paying the remaining 23 gp and finishing the weapon.
