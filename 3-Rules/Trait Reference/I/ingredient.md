# Ingredient  
*Source: Harvest Compendium p. 8*  

Ingredients are a category of item that can be crafted into something else, replacing some or all the material costs associated with crafting that item. The ingredient is consumed in the crafting process.

In crafting, ingredients are used in place of other materials. These ingredients are presented in units, the nature of which differs depending on the ingredient’s properties and how it should be stored. For instance, liquids are measured in vials, whereas powders are measured in pouches. A character must have a suitable container for these ingredients when they harvest a creature.

When crafting with ingredients, you use ingredient units equal to half the item’s level rounded up. These supplied ingredients replace the raw materials you supply to begin crafting the item. For instance, crafting a potion of resistance requires 3 units of an ingredient for a 6th-level potion, 5 units for a 10th-level potion, and so on. Crafting a flask of alchemist’s fire requires 1 unit of an ingredient for a 1st-level flask, 2 for a 3rd-level flask, and so on.

If the GM allows it, ingredients can be traded with artisans for a suitable item. If a party has gathered six vials of angel blood and wants to turn them into healing potions, but they don’t have time to craft them, they can trade the ingredients for the item directly. However, doing so requires ingredient units equal to the item’s level rather than half its level. An exception is items that are 1st-level items, where 2 ingredient units are required. Using the above example, the party can trade their six vials of angel blood for one moderate healing potion, or they can spend 4 days using the ingredients and some gold to potentially craft two moderate healing potions instead.

Ingredients cannot be crafted, only harvested.
